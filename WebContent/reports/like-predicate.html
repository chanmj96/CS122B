The LIKE Predicate


Why

We used the LIKE predicate to address situations where the user may search for a movie with parameters that are not entirely unique or specific enough. This allows the movies listing to populate with a larger amount that satisfy the basic condition placed by the user. In doing so, the user is not expected to know the exact details of a movie such as its title, director, and cast, but give sufficient information such that some information may be retrieved. Needless to say, the more specific the user, the more accurate the information returned is.

How

Our search and browse functions are the primary sections of our website that take advantage of the LIKE predicate due to their tendency to be general and not specific. More specifically, the user inputs the information they know whether that is the movie title or names of directors and cast members. The information is then passed to MySql where it is transformed into a query-friendly format. For the most part, movie titles and names make great use of the LIKE predicate whereas the year column does not. There does not seem to be purpose for the year to use it because a year cannot be "like" another year.
